Understanding Leukemia

What is Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (ALL)?

Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (ALL) is a cancer that affects white blood cells, progressing rapidly. It is most common in children but can occur in adults.

Causes & Risk Factors

  • Genetic mutations
  • Exposure to high radiation levels
  • Viral infections affecting blood cells
  • Family history of leukemia

Symptoms of Leukemia

  • Frequent infections
  • Unexplained bruising or bleeding
  • Fatigue and weakness

  • Severe weight loss
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Persistent bone pain

Types of ALL
